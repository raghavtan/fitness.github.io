45-Day Hip & Glute Strengthening Program

For a safe return to running after gluteal tendinopathy

Program Overview

This program is designed specifically for someone recovering from gluteal tendinopathy who wants to return to running after a 7-month break. It progressively builds strength in the hips, glutes, lower back, IT band, and legs while minimizing stress on the injured area.

Important Notes

  • Always prioritize proper form over repetitions.
  • Stop any exercise that causes pain (not just discomfort).
  • Continue with your warm/cold compress routine as needed.
  • Maintain good posture at your desk job (take standing breaks every hour).
  • Use the foam roller before and after exercises.

Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-15)

Focus: Activation, alignment, and basic strength

Daily Routine

  • Desk breaks: Every hour, stand up and do 10 gentle hip circles each direction.
  • Postural check: Set a timer for good sitting posture every 30 minutes.
  • Hanging practice: 2-3 times daily, practice dead hangs from your pull-up bar for 10-15 seconds.

Exercise Schedule

Days 1, 3, 5, 8, 10, 12, 15 (Strength Focus)

  1. Foam Rolling (5 minutes)
    • Roll IT band, glutes, and lower back
    • 30 seconds per area, gentle pressure
  2. Gentle Activation (5 minutes)
    • Glute bridges: 2 sets of 10 (no weight)
    • Clam shells: 2 sets of 10 each side
    • Bird dogs: 2 sets of 8 each side
  3. Basic Strength (12 minutes)
    • Wall sits: 3 sets of 25 seconds
    • Standing side leg raises: 2 sets of 10 each side
    • Modified planks (from knees): 3 sets of 25 seconds
    • Dead hangs: 2 sets of 15 seconds
    • Abdominal drawing-in maneuver: 3 sets of 10 (hold each for 5 seconds)
  4. Stretching (5 minutes)
    • Hip flexor stretch: 30 seconds each side
    • Pigeon pose: 30 seconds each side (very gentle)
    • Child's pose: 1 minute

Days 2, 4, 6, 7, 9, 11, 13, 14 (Mobility Focus)

  1. Foam Rolling (5 minutes)
    • Same as strength days
  2. Hip Mobility (10 minutes)
    • Hip circles: 2 sets of 10 each direction
    • Seated figure-four stretch: 30 seconds each side
    • Supine hip rotations: 2 sets of 8 each side
  3. Gentle Walking (10-15 minutes)
    • Focus on proper gait mechanics
    • Start with 10 minutes, work up to 15
  4. Stretching (5 minutes)
    • Same as strength days

Phase 2: Progressive Loading (Days 16-30)

Focus: Increased strength, endurance, and functional movements

Daily Routine

  • Continue with desk breaks and postural checks.
  • Add 5 minutes of light walking before beginning exercises.

Exercise Schedule

Days 16, 18, 20, 23, 25, 27, 30 (Strength Focus)

  1. Foam Rolling (5 minutes)
    • Same as Phase 1
  2. Activation (5 minutes)
    • Single-leg glute bridges: 2 sets of 8 each side
    • Side-lying leg raises with ankle weights: 2 sets of 10 each side
    • Bird dogs with 2-second holds: 2 sets of 10 each side
  3. Strength Building (20 minutes)
    • Goblet squats with 1kg dumbbells: 3 sets of 12
    • Lateral band walks: 3 sets of 15 steps each direction
    • Romanian deadlifts with 1kg dumbbells: 3 sets of 12
    • Modified side planks: 3 sets of 25 seconds each side
    • Scapular pull-ups: 3 sets of 5 (focus on shoulder blade movement)
    • Oblique crunches: 2 sets of 10 each side
    • Hollow body holds: 2 sets of 15 seconds
  4. Stretching (5 minutes)
    • Same as Phase 1 plus hamstring stretch

Days 17, 19, 21, 22, 24, 26, 28, 29 (Mobility & Light Cardio)

  1. Foam Rolling (5 minutes)
    • Same as previous
  2. Dynamic Mobility (10 minutes)
    • Walking high knees: 2 sets of 10 each leg
    • Side steps: 2 sets of 10 each direction
    • Standing hip rotations: 2 sets of 8 each direction
  3. Cardio Progression (10-20 minutes)
    • Alternate walking (2 minutes) with very light jogging (30 seconds)
    • Start with 10 minutes total, progress to 20 minutes
    • Only jog on non-consecutive days (Days 17, 21, 24, 28)
  4. Stretching (5 minutes)
    • Same as strength days

Phase 3: Running Preparation (Days 31-45)

Focus: Sport-specific movements, endurance, and initial return to running

Daily Routine

  • Continue desk breaks and add 1 minute of balance exercises when taking breaks.
  • Morning routine: 5 minutes of glute activation before starting your day.

Exercise Schedule

Days 31, 33, 35, 38, 40, 42, 45 (Strength & Plyometric Focus)

  1. Foam Rolling (5 minutes)
    • Focus on any tight areas
  2. Activation (5 minutes)
    • Single-leg glute bridges with 1kg ankle weight: 2 sets of 10 each side
    • Banded monster walks: 2 sets of 10 steps each direction
    • Bird dogs with opposite arm/leg reaches: 3 sets of 8 each side
  3. Strength & Power (25 minutes)
    • Walking lunges: 3 sets of 12 each leg
    • Step-ups onto stable platform: 3 sets of 12 each leg
    • Bridges with leg extensions: 3 sets of 10 each side
    • Full side planks: 3 sets of 35 seconds each side
    • Gentle jump squats (if pain-free): 2 sets of 10
    • Negative pull-ups: 3 sets of 3 (focus on slow lowering)
    • Hanging knee raises: 3 sets of 8
    • Pallof press: 3 sets of 10 each side
  4. Stretching (5 minutes)
    • Full routine plus IT band stretch

Days 32, 34, 36, 37, 39, 41, 43, 44 (Running Progression)

  1. Foam Rolling (5 minutes)
    • Pre-run routine
  2. Dynamic Warm-up (10 minutes)
    • Leg swings: 15 each direction, each leg
    • Walking lunges with rotation: 10 each side
    • Gentle skipping: 30 seconds
  3. Run/Walk Protocol
    • Days 32, 34: Walk 4 minutes, jog 1 minute (repeat 5 times)
    • Days 36, 37: Walk 3 minutes, jog 2 minutes (repeat 5 times)
    • Days 39, 41: Walk 2 minutes, jog 3 minutes (repeat 5 times)
    • Days 43, 44: Walk 1 minute, jog 5 minutes (repeat 4 times)
  4. Cool Down
    • Walking: 5 minutes
    • Stretching: 5 minutes (full routine)

Key Exercises Explained

Shoulder & Spinal Health

  • Dead Hangs: Hang from a pull-up bar with arms fully extended. Relax shoulders down away from ears and focus on breathing and spinal decompression. Start with shorter holds and gradually increase the time.
  • Scapular Pull-ups: Hang from the bar with arms extended. Without bending the elbows, squeeze the shoulder blades down and together, then lower back slowly. Focus on the shoulder blade movement rather than arm strength.
  • Negative Pull-ups: Start at the top of a pull-up (using a stool if necessary), then lower yourself as slowly as possible with controlled movement.

Abdominal Balance Work

  • Abdominal Drawing-In Maneuver: Lie on your back with knees bent. Inhale deeply, then exhale while drawing your navel toward your spine. Hold while breathing normally to isolate the deep transverse abdominis.
  • Pallof Press: Stand sideways to an anchor point with a resistance band at chest height. Press the band straight out, resisting rotational force, and slowly return to the center.
  • Hollow Body Hold: Lie on your back, press your lower back into the floor, extend your arms overhead and legs straight out, then lift your shoulder blades and legs slightly off the ground while maintaining core engagement.

Glute Activation

  • Glute Bridges: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Push through your heels to lift your hips while squeezing your glutes. Hold at the top for 2 seconds before lowering.
  • Clam Shells: Lie on your side with knees bent at a 45° angle and feet together. Keeping your feet in contact, lift the top knee like a clam shell, maintaining a neutral spine.

Hip Strength & Stability

  • Bird Dogs: On all fours, extend the opposite arm and leg while keeping your spine neutral. Focus on stability rather than the height of the extension.
  • Side-Lying Leg Raises: Lie on your side with the bottom leg slightly bent for stability. Raise and lower the top leg slowly. Add ankle weights in later phases if needed.

Lower Back Support

  • Modified Plank: Begin on your knees rather than your toes, ensuring a straight line from your knees to your head. Engage your core without simply holding a static position.

IT Band & Lateral Support

  • Lateral Band Walks: Place a resistance band above your knees and take small sideways steps while maintaining tension. Keep your knees slightly bent and your core engaged throughout.

Modifications for High Pain Days

If you experience a flare-up, consider:

  • Gentle swimming or water walking if available
  • Seated glute isometric holds
  • Upper body exercises only
  • Extra foam rolling and stretching

After Program Completion

By day 45, you should be able to comfortably run/walk for 20-30 minutes with minimal discomfort. Gradually increase running time while decreasing walking intervals until you can run continuously for 1-2km. Then, follow the standard 10% rule (no more than a 10% increase per week).

Important: Consult with a healthcare provider before beginning this program, especially if you’re currently under medical care for your injury.
